Introduction

The MTA Daily Ridership project was designed to explore and analyze daily ridership data from New York Cityâ€™s Metropolitan Transportation Authority (MTA). This dataset included ridership figures for various transportation modes such as Subways, Buses, Metro-North, Access-A-Ride, and Bridges & Tunnels over a defined period.

This analysis highlights the importance of data-driven insights for urban transportation management and showcases the ability to turn raw data into actionable information through analytical tools and techniques.

Objective

The primary objective of this project was to use Microsoft Excel to uncover patterns, trends, and insights in daily ridership numbers, supporting operational decision-making and resource planning. The project involved performing data cleaning, descriptive and trend analysis, correlation studies, and ridership forecasting.

Methodology

  1. Data Cleaning & Pre-processing

    • Ensured data consistency by checking for missing, duplicate, or inconsistent entries.
    • Standardized date formats and column headers for uniform analysis.
    • Handled abnormal or outlier values to improve the accuracy of insights.

  2. Excel Functions

    * Basic Functions: Used SUM(), COUNT(), AVERAGE(), MAX(), MIN() for initial descriptive statistics.

    * Conditional Functions: Applied SUMIFS(), COUNTIFS(), and IF() statements to perform filtered calculations and logical comparisons.

    * Statistical Functions: Utilized CORREL() to measure the relationship between ridership across different transportation modes.

    * Forecasting Functions: Used FORECAST.ETS() for trend-based future value predictions.

  3. PivotTables & PivotCharts

    • Created interactive PivotTables to group, summarize, and filter large data sets easily.
    • Applied PivotCharts to visually represent data trends, comparisons, and performance metrics for quick business insights.
    • Grouped data by month and category for time-based analysis.

  4. Trend Analysis & Forecasting

    • Analyzed patterns in daily, weekly, and monthly ridership data.
    • Compared pre-pandemic and post-pandemic figures to identify recovery trends.
    • Developed moving averages and linear forecasts to estimate future ridership numbers.

  5. Data Visualization

    • Built clear, interactive charts and graphs to communicate key findings effectively.
    • Applied conditional formatting to highlight important data points like highest and lowest values.

  6. Key Insights

    • Subways contributed the highest percentage of overall MTA ridership.
    • Weekday ridership was consistently higher than weekends.
    • Strong positive correlation observed between subway and bus ridership.
    • Identified specific months with peak ridership volumes.
    • Preliminary forecasts suggest a gradual post-pandemic ridership recovery.

    7. Result

    At the end of the MTA Daily Ridership Analysis project, several valuable insights were uncovered through detailed Excel-based analysis:

    1. Subways accounted for the highest share of total MTA ridership, contributing a significant percentage compared to other transportation modes.
    2. Weekday ridership was consistently higher than weekend ridership across all modes, confirming typical commuter behavior in New York City.
    3. The strongest positive correlation was observed between Subway and Bus ridership, indicating parallel usage patterns likely influenced by daily commuting trends.
    4. Identified the specific day and month with the highest overall MTA ridership, aiding operational planning for high-demand periods.
    5. Post-pandemic ridership trends showed a gradual recovery, with Subways and Buses leading the recovery rates.
    6. Forecast models predicted an upward trend in Subway ridership over the next three months, suggesting a continued rebound in public transit usage.
    7. Created interactive PivotTables, charts, and dashboards for easy visualization and reporting of key metrics and trends.

    This project demonstrated how structured data analysis can provide actionable insights for transportation management, policy-making, and resource optimization.
