Restaurant Menu Items & Order Analysis using SQL

Restaurant Menu Items & Order Analysis

Introduction

This project focused on analyzing a restaurant’s sales performance by exploring data from menu items and order details tables stored in a database. The objective was to evaluate customer preferences, menu pricing trends, and ordering behaviors using structured SQL queries.

Objective

To extract business insights about menu item popularity, category-wise pricing, high-value orders, and ordering patterns by querying a restaurant’s transactional database.

Methodology

Database Familiarization:

  1. Reviewed the data dictionary to understand table structures, columns, and relationships.
  2. Explored the menu_items and order_details tables to examine available data.

Menu Analysis:

Retrieved the full list of menu items.

Calculated:

  1. Total number of items on the menu.
  2. Least and most expensive items.
  3. Number of Italian dishes on the menu.
  4. Least and most expensive Italian dishes.
  5. Average price per dish category.

Order Data Exploration:

  1. The date range of order data.
  2. Total number of orders placed.
  3. Total number of items sold within the date range.
  4. Orders with the highest number of items.
  5. Number of orders containing more than 12 items.

Combining Datasets (Joins):

Performed an INNER JOIN on menu_items and order_details tables to merge order information with menu details.

  1. Identified:

    • Least and most ordered menu items and their categories.
    • Top 5 orders with the highest spend.
    • Detailed breakdown of the highest spend order.
    • Key trends from the top 5 highest spend orders (items ordered most frequently in high-value orders, popular categories, average order size).

    2. Results

    Identified the most and least popular menu items based on order count.

    Revealed that certain categories, especially Italian and Appetizer items, had higher average pricing.

    Discovered specific dishes driving top sales and high-value orders.

    Highlighted trends from top orders — e.g., high spend orders often included multiple premium-priced items and a mix of categories.

    Delivered actionable insights on menu optimization and pricing strategy based on ordering behavior and revenue contribution.
